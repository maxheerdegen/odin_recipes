Oyakodon

Description

Oyakodon is a classic Japanese dish translating to "parent and child rice bowl," featuring chicken and egg simmered in a sweet and savory broth. Quick and heartwarming!

Ingredients

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken thighs, thinly sliced
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 4 eggs, beaten
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons mirin (sweet rice wine)
  • 2 tablespoons sake (Japanese rice wine)
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 cup dashi (Japanese soup stock) or chicken broth
  • Cooked rice for serving
  • Chopped green onions for garnish

Steps

  1. In a skillet, combine soy sauce, mirin, sake, sugar, and dashi. Bring to a simmer over medium heat.
  2. Add sliced chicken and onions to the simmering broth and cook until the chicken is no longer pink and the onions are tender.
  3. Pour the beaten eggs evenly over the chicken and onions. Cover and let it simmer until the eggs are just set.
  4. Serve the oyakodon over a bowl of steamed rice and garnish with chopped green onions.
